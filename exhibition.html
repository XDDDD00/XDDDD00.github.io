Pattern's Name

Common patterns found on bronze wares include:

  • Geometric patterns: Such as spirals, diamonds, and grids, often used to represent order and harmony.
  • Zoomorphic patterns: Depictions of animals, such as dragons, phoenixes, and tigers, often symbolizing power, strength, and auspiciousness.
  • Human figures: Representations of human beings, often engaged in ceremonial or mythological activities.
  • Mythological creatures: Depictions of mythical creatures, such as chimeras and qilins, believed to possess supernatural powers.
  • Calligraphy and inscriptions: Inscriptions of characters, often conveying religious, political, or personal messages.

These patterns were not merely decorative elements but also served as a means of conveying meaning and status. The choice of patterns often reflected the social standing, religious beliefs, and artistic preferences of the individuals or groups who commissioned the bronze wares.
